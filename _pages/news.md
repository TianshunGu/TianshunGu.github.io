## 2026

- **Mar 16, 2026**  
  🎤 Invited by Dr. Yan Zhang, I took part in the GISChat Seminar Series (Episode 26), *Host × GIS: Building and Managing My Own Platform*. Using *Urban Geography Illumination*—a public science communication initiative on urban geography—as a case study, I shared how the platform was developed, what I have learned from running it, and a few thoughts on its future direction.  
  [👉 Full article (WeChat, in Chinese)](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/RIPRQD_jyImL2txs8eEsyg)

- **Feb 13, 2026**  
  📄 Our study, *Attribution of the 2022 Asian Spatially Compound Heatwave-Flooding Event to Atmospheric Circulation, La Niña and Anthropogenic Warming*, was published in *Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres*. This work examines the drivers behind the spatial co-occurrence of heatwave and flooding extremes across Asia and contributes to a better understanding of compound climate risks under a warming background.  
  [👉 Read more (WeChat article, in Chinese)](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/QU4tjlMzq9cO1PhKhuL4yw)

- **Jan 12, 2026**  
  📄 Our study, *Steeper Spatiotemporal Distribution of Extreme Precipitation Intensity in Urban than Rural Regions*, was published in *Journal of Hydrology*. This work explores how urbanization intensifies the spatiotemporal clustering of extreme precipitation and offers new evidence on how cities reshape the persistence and organization of heavy rainfall events.  
  [👉 Read more (WeChat article, in Chinese)](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/qq3rpITchb5k2GEMJwSpow)

## 2024

- **Dec 2024**  
  📄 Our study, *Urban Landscape Patterns and Residents’ Perceptions of Safety under Extreme City Flood Disasters*, was published in *Ecological Indicators*. The paper explores how urban landscape structure shapes residents’ safety perception under extreme flood conditions and contributes to the growing literature on flood resilience and human-environment interactions.
